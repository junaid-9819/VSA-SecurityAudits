VSA Business Growth

Leverage the Vendor Security Alliance (VSA) to grow your client base for vendor and third-party audit business

Enhance your service offerings, build credibility, and attract more clients by strategically utilizing VSA's standardized frameworks and tools.

As an Information Security and consulting firm, you can absolutely leverage the Vendor Security Alliance (VSA) to grow your client base for vendor and third-party audit business. The VSA offers tools, credibility, and a standardized framework that can enhance your service offerings and appeal to potential clients. Here's how you can strategically use the VSA to attract more business:

1
Utilize VSA's Standardized Tools

The VSA provides free, annually updated questionnaires—such as the VSA-Full and VSA-Core—that are widely recognized for assessing vendor cybersecurity practices. By incorporating these into your audit services:

  • Demonstrate Expertise: Show clients you're aligned with industry-leading standards used by major companies like Uber, Airbnb, and Dropbox.
  • Streamline Processes: Offer a familiar, efficient assessment process that clients may already trust, reducing their onboarding friction.
  • Differentiate Your Firm: Position yourself as a consultancy that leverages proven, standardized tools rather than proprietary or ad-hoc methods, which can build confidence in your approach.
2
Target VSA Members and Non-Members
  • VSA Members: These companies (e.g., tech giants and growing firms) are already invested in the VSA ecosystem. They may need external consultants to conduct audits using VSA questionnaires or to interpret results. Pitch your services as a way to offload their vendor assessment workload or enhance their compliance efforts.
  • Non-Members: Many organizations outside the VSA want to align with best practices but lack the resources or expertise. You can offer to implement VSA-based assessments for them, helping them meet the same standards as VSA members and prepare for partnerships with larger firms.
3
Enhance Credibility and Marketing
  • Association with VSA: Mentioning your use of VSA tools in your marketing materials, proposals, and website lends instant credibility. It signals that your firm is plugged into a respected coalition focused on third-party risk management.
  • Thought Leadership: Create content (blogs, webinars, whitepapers) about how you use VSA frameworks to solve real-world vendor security challenges. This positions you as a knowledgeable player in the space and attracts clients seeking guidance.
  • Certifications and Partnerships: While the VSA doesn't offer formal certifications for consultants, you could explore becoming a recognized partner or contributor to their initiatives, further boosting your reputation.
4
Offer Value-Added Services

Clients often need more than just questionnaire completion—they want actionable insights and ongoing support. Use the VSA framework as a foundation to:

  • Gap Analysis: Identify weaknesses in vendor security based on VSA results and offer remediation consulting.
  • Continuous Monitoring: Provide ongoing vendor risk management beyond one-off audits, using VSA benchmarks as a baseline.
  • Training and Implementation: Help clients adopt VSA processes internally, training their teams or integrating the framework into their procurement workflows.
5
Tap Into Third-Party Risk Management Demand

The growing emphasis on third-party risk—driven by regulations like GDPR, CCPA, and high-profile breaches—means businesses are actively seeking audit and consulting services. By aligning with the VSA:

  • Address Pain Points: Many companies struggle with vetting vendors consistently. Offer VSA-based audits as a scalable, reliable solution.
  • Expand Scope: Use VSA assessments as an entry point to upsell broader cybersecurity services, like penetration testing or policy development.

Practical Steps to Get Started

  • Download and Master the VSA Questionnaires: Familiarize yourself with the VSA-Full (in-depth cybersecurity focus) and VSA-Core (essentials plus privacy). Tailor your audit methodology around them.
  • Build a Pitch: Create a service package that highlights how your VSA-based audits save time, ensure compliance, and mitigate risks. Target industries like tech, finance, or healthcare, where vendor security is critical.
  • Network with VSA Ecosystem: Attend industry events where VSA members or affiliates might be present, or engage with their community online to build relationships.
  • Showcase Success: After completing VSA-based audits, ask clients for testimonials or case studies (with permission) to demonstrate your impact.

Potential Challenges to Consider

  • Competition: Other firms may also use VSA tools, so emphasize your unique value (e.g., faster turnaround, deeper analysis, or sector-specific expertise).
  • Client Education: Some prospects may not know the VSA. Be prepared to explain its benefits concisely and why it matters.
  • Scope Limitation: The VSA questionnaires are robust but not exhaustive. Be ready to supplement them with additional frameworks (e.g., NIST, ISO 27001) to meet complex client needs.

By positioning your firm as a VSA-savvy consultancy, you can tap into a growing market of businesses needing third-party audits, enhance your reputation, and deliver tangible value—all of which can translate into more clients for your vendor security and audit business.
